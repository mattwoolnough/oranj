2. Scope

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, hold, use, and disclose personal information when you interact with:

  • our website and online forms;
  • our sales and support communications;
  • our Facebook and Messenger-based engagement workflows.

3. Data We Collect

We collect two broad categories of information:

  1. Information you provide directly (such as name, email, phone, company, and enquiry details).
  2. Information collected automatically or through platforms (such as device/browser data, cookies, PSID, thread identifiers, and interaction metadata).

Depending on your interaction with us, we may collect:

  • contact and account details;
  • service enquiry and conversation content (including Messenger messages and timestamps);
  • lead, pipeline, and engagement information;
  • billing or transaction-related information where required;
  • compliance, consent, and audit records;
  • technical and diagnostic logs.

4. How We Collect Data

We collect personal information when you:

  1. submit forms, book calls, send messages, or email us;
  2. interact with our Facebook Page, Messenger entry points, or connected campaigns;
  3. use our website (through cookies and analytics tools);
  4. interact with our team during delivery, support, or onboarding.

We may also receive information from trusted third parties (for example, platform providers, analytics tools, CRM systems, and referral partners) where permitted by law.

5. Why We Use Data

We use data to:

  1. provide requested services, resources, and support;
  2. manage customer relationships, qualification, and service delivery;
  3. operate and improve our website, systems, and communications;
  4. maintain security, prevent misuse, and enforce internal policies;
  5. meet legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.

We do not sell personal information to data brokers, and we do not use personal information for purposes that are inconsistent with this policy.

6. Legal Bases (Where Applicable)

Where required, our handling of personal information is based on:

  1. your consent;
  2. performance of a contract or pre-contract steps;
  3. our legitimate interests in running and improving our services;
  4. compliance with legal obligations.

Where practical, you may choose to interact with us anonymously or by pseudonym. In some cases, that may limit our ability to provide full services.

7. Cookies, Analytics and Similar Technologies

We use cookies and similar tools to operate our website, understand usage patterns, and improve performance.

You can control cookies via browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect site functionality.

8. Direct Marketing

We may send service updates, event information, and marketing communications where permitted by law, including the Spam Act 2003 (Cth).

You can opt out at any time by using unsubscribe links or contacting us at contact@oranj.com.au.

9. Disclosure of Personal Information

We may disclose personal information to:

  1. technology, hosting, communications, and analytics providers;
  2. professional advisers (for example legal, accounting, and compliance advisers);
  3. platform providers including Meta where needed for Messenger workflows;
  4. authorities, courts, or regulators where required or authorised by law;
  5. successor entities in a merger, acquisition, or business transfer.

We seek to disclose only what is reasonably necessary and use contractual and operational controls with service providers where appropriate.

10. Overseas Disclosure

Some service providers we use may store or process personal information outside Australia (for example in the United States or other jurisdictions where our vendors operate).

When this occurs, we take reasonable steps to ensure overseas recipients handle personal information in a way that is consistent with Australian privacy requirements.

11. Security

We take reasonable steps to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access, modification, or disclosure.

Controls may include encryption, access controls, monitoring, secure configuration, and audit logging.

12. Retention

We keep personal information only for as long as needed for business and legal purposes, then securely delete or de-identify it.

Category Retention Target
Account/contact, Facebook identifiers, conversation content, lead/pipeline data Active + 12 months inactive archive
AI scoring evidence, consent/compliance data, operator actions/audit logs 24 months
Technical logs 90 days (unless incident hold applies)

Where practical, verified deletion requests are targeted for completion within 30 days.

13. AI Privacy Controls

  1. AI inputs are limited to conversation excerpts and required lead metadata.
  2. We aim to exclude high-risk personal information that is not needed for the task.
  3. AI outputs are assistive; human review is used for sensitive decisions and communications.
  4. Compliance, opt-out, and deletion controls apply across AI-enabled workflows.
